The Gittleson shrug emphasizes the contraction of the upper trapezius muscle by isolating the muscle and working it in a one-way fashion. Allow your loaded side shoulder to drop down as far as possible. This puts a stretch on your left upper trap and, because of the way you’re leaning, it also puts an intense isometric contraction in your right upper trap. Although it seems like a one-way exercise, an isometric contraction occurs on the other side as one side moves.
